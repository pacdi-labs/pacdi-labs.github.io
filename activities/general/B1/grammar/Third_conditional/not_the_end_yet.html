Not the end yet...

Conclusion

Let's review what we have learned.

   1. The purpose or function of the THIRD CONDITIONAL is to express past wishes and unreal events = actions that already happened and we are thinking that it could have been different (result) if we had done something else (condition). 

   2. The easiest way not to get confused is to place the "if" in one clause and the "modal+have" in the other.

   3. The negation goes after the auxiliary had in the if clause and after the modal in the main clause.

   4. Remember that the punctuation is also important: put a comma when we start with the "if" clause, and don't forget the final period.

I hope that you've learned everything you needed to know.  Keep practicing to become an expert on this topic!
