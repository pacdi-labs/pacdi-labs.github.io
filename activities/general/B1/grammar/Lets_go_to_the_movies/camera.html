Camera...

 

It really is very impressive to see all the people who are needed to make a film and how each one performs a very important role in order to produce a successful movie. Everyone needs the support of another person to be able to do his or her own part. The director needs the help of competent and committed actors to film each scene. Actors require make-up artists who design and apply the makeup for each actor/actress.  Make-up professionals use make-up to do their job, and someone else has to create the products. But not just that, stuntmen risk their lives doing the dangerous scenes so that the actors do not get hurt.  Every role is fundamental,it works like a puzzle, if a piece is missing it will not be complete.

Just as the actors need the help of someone else to do something for them, you need the help of others as well! Have you ever stopped to think about the services you obtain from others? Could you repair your own car? Could you fix your washing machine? If you couldn't or if you don't want to do these things yourself, you need to get someone to do them for you. 

When you want to express that someone else does something for you, the causative verbs are used. The English verbs let, make, have, and get are called causative verbs because they cause something else to happen. This form can be used in the different verb tenses and obviously the rules for each tense must be respected. 

How To Use Causative Verbs In English?

LET = permit something to happen.

Grammatical structure:

  • LET + PERSON/THING + VERB (base form)

Example:

  • I don’t let my kids watch violent movies.  (Present simple)
  • The director let the actors take a rest after every scene. (Present simple)
  • Oops! I wasn’t paying attention while cooking because I was watching my favorite movie at the same time, and I let the food burn.   (Past simple)

MAKE = force or require someone to take an action.

Grammatical structure:

  • MAKE + PERSON + VERB (base form)

Examples:

  • Angelina Jolie makes her children behave properly.   (Present simple)
  • The director of the film will not make the film crew wear uniforms.  (Future simple)
  • After Billy broke my TV, his parents made him pay for it. (Past simple)

HAVE = give someone else the responsibility to do something.

Grammatical structure:

  • HAVE + PERSON + VERB (base form)

Examples of grammatical structure:

  • I’ll have my best friend buy the tickets for the movie. (Future simple)
  • The make-up artist had the actress choose the color of the shadow for her eyes. (Past simple)
  • The actors have the stuntmen perform the dangerous scenes.  (Present simple)

GET = convince/encourage someone to do something.

Grammatical structure:

  • GET + PERSON + TO + VERB (infinitive form)

Examples:

  • How can we get the actors to arrive on time? (Present simple)
  • Jennifer Aniston hates housework; her family can never get her to wash the dishes.  (Present simple)

If you pay close attention to the previous examples, you can notice that all of them are in active voice, active voice is used when the subject is the doer of the action. However, causative verbs can also be used in passive voice. Passive voice is used when the subject is the receiver of the action. The passive is used in these cases because we don’t always know who the doer of the action is. Only the verbs have and get are used in passive voice (Get is less formal). 

  • The teacher had the students do the assignment. (Active Form)
    (The teacher caused the students to do the assignment).
  • The teacher had the assignment done. (Passive Form)
    (We don’t know exactly who did the assignment, so this is like a passive)

Grammatical structure

  • HAVE/GET + THING + PAST PARTICIPLE OF VERB

Examples:

  • Jonny Deep had/got his teeth whitened; his smile looks great!  (Past simple) 
  • My cam recorder is broken; I need to have/get it repaired.  (Present simple)

       

True-False Question

Analyze the sentences and decide if they are correct or not. Choose True if the sentence is correct, choose false if the sentence is wrong.

Example: My teacher made me apologize for what I had said.       TRUE          FALSE 

(The sentence is correct.)

Question 1

1. Ana let me to drive her new car.

Feedback

False

The sentence is incorrect. Let is never used with the infinitive form of the verb. The correct sentence should be: Ana let me drive her new car.

Question 2

2. Did somebody make you wear that ugly hat?

Feedback

True

The sentence is correct, it respects the rules of simple past tense.

Question 3

3. He got the students prepare a presentation.

Feedback

False

The sentence is incorrect because the verb is not in infinitive. He got the students to prepare a presentation is the correct sentence.

Question 4

4. She had the car washed.

Feedback

True

The sentence is correct.

Question 5

5. She made her children do their homework

Feedback

True

The sentence does not have any mistake.

Question 6

6. Will your parents to let you go to the party?

Feedback

False

The sentence is incorrect, the causative verb let is not used in infinitive form

Question 7

7. My father has the mechanic check the brakes regularly. 

Feedback

True

The sentence respects causative form's rules.

Question 8

8. We had the gardener trims the bushes.

Feedback

False

The verb cannot be conjugated. The correct sentence is: We had the gardener trim the bushes.

Question 9

9. Please dad, let me go to the concert!

Feedback

True

The sentence is correct.

Question 10

10.  He can't to make you do anything you don’t want to do.

Feedback

False

The causative verb make is never used in infinitive form.
