Non-restrictive relative clauses or Non-defining relative clauses

As the name suggests, non-defining relative clauses tell us more about someone or something, but the information in these clauses does not help us to define what we are talking about. Therefore, they give extra information. 

If the non-restrictive relative clauses were removed from the sentence, the sentence would still be grammatically correct and the meaning would not change, we would only have less details.

Non-defining relative clauses are composed of a relative pronoun, a verb, and optional elements such as the subject or object of the verb.  

Commas are always used to separate non-restrictive relative clauses from the rest of the sentence.

In non-defining relative clauses we can never use that.

Examples: 

Her grandmother, who is dead now, came from United States. 

I've met Susan, whose husband works in Aguascalientes.

His car, which is blue, is very expensive.

The name of the city, where she lives, is Puebla.

February, when Valentine's Day is celebrated, is the windiest month in some places.
