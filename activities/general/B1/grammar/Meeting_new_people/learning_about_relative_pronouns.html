Learning about relative pronouns!

Relative clauses

Read the information to know about relative clauses, then answer the following activity. 

When we meet new people, when we visit a new place or when we learn something new about an object, we can use relative clauses to help us to express our ideas. We use relative clauses to describe or give extra information about something we have already mentioned.

What are relative clauses? 

A clause is a group of words containing a verb. Relative clauses are a way of giving more information about a person, thing, place, event, etc. We often use them to avoid repeating information. A relative clause is introduced by a relative pronoun.

What are relative pronouns?

A relative pronoun is a word that is used to refer to nouns mentioned previously, whether they are people, places, things, animals, or ideas. 

They are called relative pronouns because they always relate back to something or someone else.

A relative pronoun replaces the personal pronoun (I, you, he, they...)as the subject of the clause.

Relative pronoun Usage Example
who Refers to people.  This is the boy who broke the window. 
which Refers to animals and things. She gave me a cat which was very small. 
that Refers to people, animals and things. The activity that I am doing is interesting. 
whose Refers to possession.  She is the woman whose sister lives in Mexico City. 
when Refers to time.  He remembers the day when we met. 
where Refers to places. London is the city where my friend is living now. 

Relative pronouns can be used to join two sentences about the same person or thing.
