Introduction

You are what you eat!

  • Write a list of all the food you ate yesterday in your notebook. Be honest and try not to forget any little snacks. 
BREAKFAST  LUNCH DINNER SNACKS

  • Have a look at your list and answer some questions in your notebook. 

1. Is there anything that appears several times on your list? If so, what is it?

2. What do you understand by the saying "you are what you eat"?

3. Put a tick next to all the healthy food on your list and cross next to the unhealthy food. 

You are what you eat! This phrase is literally true. Nutrients from the food you eat provide the foundation of the structure, function and integrity of every little cell in your body, from your skin and hair to your muscles, bones, digestive and immune systems.
