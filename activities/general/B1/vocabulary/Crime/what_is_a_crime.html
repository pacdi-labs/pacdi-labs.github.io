What is a crime?

Read the information about the causes of crimes, if you find any unknown word, you can look it up in the dictionary. 

Before we can discuss the causes of crime, it is first necessary to determine what we mean by the word “crime”. The legal definition of crime refers to “an action or omission which constitutes an offence” and involves some sort of punishment. Crime is one of the most prevalent problems in any society. People live in fear and are even afraid to leave their houses. It is a constant concern of whether you will be the next victim or not. 

 As society changes, some actions which used to be criminal are no longer so. Likewise some actions which were legal can become prohibited. Laws are made by the politicians we elect democratically. In a democratic society someone charged with a crime has the opportunity to defend him/herself. He or she will be deemed innocent until proven guilty by a criminal court. Punishments traditionally reflect the seriousness of the crime, the most serious are those which involve violence and/or loss of life.

In today’s world, people commit criminal offenses, get away with them and prosecute other people for the same crimes they committed. People commit crimes due to various reasons. The causes of crime are complex, even though we can have an idea to explain why these horrible situations happen. 

Overpopulation is considered one of the main causes of crime. For several years population has been increasing faster than many vital non-renewable and renewable resources. This means the amount of these resources per person is declining, in spite of modern technology. The more people on the planet, the less resources there will be.

Overpopulation leads to poverty, which is another reason is conduct to crime. The rich are continuing to get richer, and the poor get poorer. Living in poverty can get frustrating making people do unethical acts such as prostitution, and corruption among many. In our cities the highest delinquency and crime rates are in the slum areas, for perfectly understandable reasons. Here are the poorest housing conditions, the largest families, the smallest incomes, the worst health problems, the largest number of homes with parents who have themselves been in trouble with the law, and the least supervision over children. In these places gangs are formed and children learn the evil side of the world too early. 

However, it cannot be generalized, many slum children grow up in a decent way. Here is when education plays a very important role. Not just the education received in the schools but the values learned at home, the love and the attention they receive. Family violence and other issues are also related to crime in many ways.

Other factors that are important to be considered to know the causes of crime are mental health, drugs and unemployment. Depression is also a major cause of crime. Other than depression, people with grave mental disorders also end up committing crimes. A person under depression or some other serious mental disorder can also easily cause harm to themselves. Drugs are a big problem too. A drug addict ends up committing crimes to raise money for their habits. Besides that there are also a large number of people that are involved in the drug trade: cartels, politicians and even the governments. 

Besides the list of the factors already mentioned to be the causes of a crime, it cannot be just that, other factors can be involved. Every crime is different, just as every criminal.
