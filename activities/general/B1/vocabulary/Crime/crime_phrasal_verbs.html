Crime phrasal verbs

In order to understand better crime contexts you have to learn some phrasal vrbs that are related to. 

PHRASAL VERB MEANING  EXAMPLE
beat up  to brutally attack and injure someone

The police officers beat up the protesters. 
break into to illegally enter someone's house

The girl saw him braking into her neighbor's house.
lock up to be put in jail

My best friend was locked up last year because he stole a car.
break out of  to escape from captivity

My lawyer did not help Juan break out of prison.
walk off with to steal or take something

 

The theft walked off with the money and the police did not notice. 
hold up to take money from a person or a business with a threat of violence. 

 

The man who held up the bank died after the police man shot him.
tip off to inform the police about a criminal or a crime

Greg tipped police off on his car phone about a suspect drunk driver
let off te release someone with little or no punishment

I’ll let you off this time, but don’t do it again.
take someone in  to deceive or trick someone 

 

He took me in completely with his sad story.
put someone up to  to encourage or convince someone to do something stupid or dangerous

Did your friends put you up to this?’ ‘No, it was my own idea
