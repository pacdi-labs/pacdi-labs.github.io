Second

Check the vocabulary list and definitions, click on the terms in bold to practice listening to their pronunciation. This way, when you listen to the podcast, you will be able to easily identify them!

In your portafolio, write down their pronunciation as you hear it. 👂🏼

  1. Regarding: with respect to; concerning; about.
  2. Approach: mention; analyze; talk about.
  3. Obesity: the condition of being grossly fat or overweight.
  4. Loaded: informal expression that means “full of”.
  5. Worldwide: extending or reaching throughout the world.
  6. Drive-through (drive-thru): a place or facility where one can be served without leaving one's car.
  7. Tends: to regularly or frequently behave in a particular way or to have a certain characteristic.
  8. Morbidity: the rate of disease in a population.
  9. Mortality: death, specially on a large scale.
  10. Raised: more intense than usual; higher.
  11. Doing a number on: informal way of saying to treat harshly; to damage.
  12. Sides: a food item that accompanies the entrée or main course at a meal.
  13. Carbohydrates: one of several substances (such as sugar or starch) that provide the body with energy; foods containing these substances such as bread, potatoes, pasta, and rice.
  14. Fiber: the part of food that is eaten but not digested, it passes through the body and is excreted as waste.
  15. Cholesterol: a substance containing a lot of fat that is found in the body tissue and blood of all animals, it is thought to be part of the cause of heart disease if there is too much of it.
  16. Weight gain: it is an increase in body weight. This can involve an increase in muscle mass, fat deposits, and excess fluids such as water or other factors.
  17. Insulin: a hormone in the body that controls the amount of sugar in the blood.
  18. Type 2 diabetes: it is a problem with your body that causes blood glucose (sugar) levels to rise higher than normal. This is also called hyperglycemia.
  19. Asthma: a medical condition that makes breathing difficult by causing the air passages to become narrow or blocked.
  20. Shortness of breath: the feeling of not getting enough air when you are breathing.
  21. Pounds: a unit for measuring weight.
  22. Bone density: a measurement of the amount of calcium and other minerals in a segment of bone.
  23. Muscle mass: white tissue connected to the skeleton through tendons which generates corporal movement.
  24. To price: informal expression to give a value or worth to something.
