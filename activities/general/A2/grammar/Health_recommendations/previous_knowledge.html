Before we continue...

Before you learn the uses of  the verb should , let's make sure you understand what a modal verb is. Do you remember how to use the verb can? Well, can is a modal verb. 

A modal verb is a verb used with another verb to express an idea, such as possibility, that is not expressed by the main verb.

Do not forget! 

Can is a modal verb, to create affirmative sentences we follow this structure: 

Subject  +  can  +  * verb  +  complement.  

* it is not necessary to put to before the verb. 

 

Turn the given sentences into negative sentences or questions. The phrases  tell you what to do. (Remember to use capital letters at the beginning of the sentences) Do not use contractions.

EXAMPLE:

He can correct the mistakes. → negative sentence

ANSWER:

He cannot correct the mistakes.

Do not forget!

Can is a modal verb, to form sentences in negative you need: 

Subject  +  cannot  (can't) +  *verb  +  complement.

To make questions or interrogative sentences you follow this structure: 

Can  +  subject  +  *verb  +  complement?

* The verb needs to be in the base form
