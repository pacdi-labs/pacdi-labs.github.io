Do not forget! 

Do is an auxiliary verb in English. 

We use do to make negatives and question forms.

Question (?) form

To make the question form of most main verbs in present simple, we use do, does (he, she, it) followed by the subject and the main verb:

Do you playfootball?

Does he play football?

Negative form

The negative of the present simple of all main verbs (except for be) is made with auxiliary do + not, which is shortened to don’t (do not), doesn’t (does not). We use the short forms in everyday informal language, and the full forms in more formal situations:

I don’t want to wait for a bus. Let’s get a taxi.

Jack doesn’t live in the town centre. He’s out in the suburbs.
